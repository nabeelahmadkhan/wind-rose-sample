Wind Rose Chart - Hour Frequency

Overview: The Wind Rose Chart is a visual tool used to depict the distribution of wind speeds and directions over a specified period, typically in hours. This chart provides insights into the wind patterns and behavior in a particular region, helping in various meteorological and environmental analyses.

Data Representation:

  • Wind Speed Ranges: The data is categorized into different wind speed ranges (e.g., 0.0-0.2 m/s, 0.3-1.5 m/s, etc.).
  • Direction Frequencies: For each wind speed range, the frequency of wind coming from specific directions (N, NE, E, SE, S, SW, W, NW) is recorded in hours.

Chart Features:

  • Polar Coordinate System: The chart uses a polar coordinate system, where directions are represented along the angle axis, and the frequency in hours is represented along the radius axis.
  • Stacked Bars: Each wind speed range is represented as a stacked bar, making it easy to compare the distribution of different wind speeds from various directions.
  • Interactive Tooltips: Hovering over a segment of the chart displays detailed information about the wind speed range, direction, and frequency in hours.

By representing wind data in hours, the Wind Rose Chart provides a clear and intuitive way to analyze how often winds of certain speeds occur from different directions, aiding in comprehensive environmental and meteorological assessments.
